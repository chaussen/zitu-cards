Print

A4 stock at 100% scale, 120 gsm or heavier. 米字格 grids print at hairline weight so they don't compete with the character.

贰

Duplex

Two-sided · flip on long edge. Print landscape A4 — 2 portrait cards side-by-side per sheet. Back cards print in the same column order as fronts (no mirroring needed).

叁

Cut & sort

Cut along the vertical dashed centre line and trim the outer margins. Cards finish at approx. 135×190mm (portrait A5). Stack by tone, by formation type, or by HSK level.

肆

Quiz with the front. Study with the back. Drill on the practice sheet. Display the poster. One pack a week.

人
Two strokes — a person walking, seen in profile. The sweeping left stroke is the torso and leading leg; the right is the trailing leg. Compressed to 亻 on the left of person-related characters.
left leg 丿 + right leg ㇏ = 人

山
Three peaks rising from a single baseline — one of the least-changed characters from oracle bone to today. 山 is its own radical, appearing in characters for peaks, ridges, and rugged terrain.
left peak + tallest peak + right peak = 山

How to study three passes
Pass 1 · See & say. Flip front-only. Read aloud — character, pinyin, meaning.
Pass 2 · Recall. Cover the pinyin with your thumb. Say the tone before flipping.
Pass 3 · Write. Trace into the practice sheet's blank 米字格 from memory, then compare.
