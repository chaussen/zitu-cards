水
A central downward current flanked by two droplets — water seen in cross-section. Its contracted radical 氵 (three drops) marks over 1,000 water-related characters.
central current 亅 + droplets 丶丶 = 水

人
Two strokes — a person walking, seen in profile. The sweeping left stroke is the torso and leading leg; the right is the trailing leg. Compressed to 亻 on the left of person-related characters.
left leg 丿 + right leg ㇏ = 人

山
Three peaks rising from a single baseline — one of the least-changed characters from oracle bone to today. 山 is its own radical, appearing in characters for peaks, ridges, and rugged terrain.
left peak + tallest peak + right peak = 山

日
A circle with a dot at centre — the sun's disc and its blinding core. Squared off in clerical script. The central line distinguishes 日 (sun) from 口 (mouth), which is just an open box.
disc ⼝ + core 一 = 日

木
A cross-section of a standing tree: the horizontal stroke is the branches, the vertical is the trunk, and the two lower strokes are the roots. 木 appears in nearly every character related to wood or plants.
branches 一 + trunk 丨 + roots 丿㇏ = 木

Cultural note 木 loses its roots and gains a dot to become 本 (běn) — origin, basis, root. The dot marks where the root is. Adding a horizontal stroke at the top gives 末 (mò) — tip, end.

月
A crescent laid on its back, with two strokes inside indicating the moon's phases. 月 also doubles as the 'flesh/body' radical 肉 in many anatomical characters — same shape, different meaning.
crescent ⺆ + phases 二 = 月

心
Oracle bone script drew the actual heart: a curved body with three blood vessels branching off. The hook is the heart itself; the dots are the aortic branches. As a radical (忄) it marks emotional states.
heart body ⺄ + vessels 丶丶丶 = 心

Cultural note In Chinese medicine and philosophy, 心 is the seat of consciousness — not just emotion but thought, intention, and moral will. The mind-heart distinction that shapes Western thought does not apply.
